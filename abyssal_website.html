Our Mission

Mapping the Unmapped

More than 80% of our oceans remain unexplored. ABYSSAL exists to change that — deploying state-of-the-art remotely operated vehicles, AI-assisted cataloguing, and long-duration submersibles into the hadal zone where pressure exceeds 1,000 atmospheres.

◎

Full-Spectrum Mapping

Sub-centimetre resolution sonar mapping of unexplored ocean floor terrain.

⬡

Biodiversity Cataloguing

Environmental DNA sampling combined with visual AI for real-time species identification.

◈

Climate Research

Deep current monitoring to model the ocean's role in global heat distribution.

Engineering

The Nereid
ROV Platform

Our flagship remotely operated vehicle represents a decade of iteration. Built to withstand 16,000 psi of hydrostatic pressure, Nereid III carries a full scientific payload including paired 8K cameras, manipulator arms with sub-gram force sensing, and a novel real-time fibre-optic tether system.

Operating depth11,000m rated
Pressure rating16,000 psi
Camera systemDual 8K · 360°
Battery endurance72 hours
Manipulator DOF7-axis · 4kg payload
Tether length15,000m fibre-optic
Sample capacity32 individual bio-containers

Dive into History

2017 · Founding

ABYSSAL is established

Founded in Lisbon by a team of oceanographers and engineers with a shared belief that the deep ocean holds keys to understanding Earth's climate, origins of life, and biodiversity.

800m
2019 · First Expedition

Azores Seamount Survey

Our inaugural mission discovered a cold-water coral reef system on Condor Seamount, with 11 species new to the Azores region. The Nereid I ROV performs flawlessly across 14 dives.

3,800m
2021 · Breakthrough

New species confirmed: A. bathyalis

DNA sequencing confirms a new species of snailfish — Abyssallus bathyalis — setting a new record for the deepest confirmed vertebrate in the Atlantic basin.

10,924m
2024 · Record Dive

Challenger Deep, Mariana VII

The Nereid III reaches full ocean depth in Challenger Deep, recovering sediment cores and biological samples from the deepest point on Earth while streaming 8K footage live.
